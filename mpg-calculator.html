Frequently Asked Questions

How is MPG calculated?
MPG (miles per gallon) = miles driven ÷ gallons of fuel used. To measure accurately: fill your tank completely, reset your trip odometer, drive normally, then fill up again. Divide the miles on the odometer by the gallons needed to fill back to full.
What is a good MPG?
For gasoline cars: 30+ MPG is good, 40+ MPG is excellent. The average new car gets about 27 MPG (2024). Hybrids typically achieve 45–60 MPG. EVs are rated in MPGe (miles per gallon equivalent), where 100+ MPGe is common. Trucks and SUVs typically average 15–22 MPG.
Why does my actual MPG differ from the EPA estimate?
EPA estimates are measured under controlled laboratory conditions. Real-world MPG is affected by driving style, speed, terrain, weather, AC use, tire inflation, cargo load, and vehicle age. Most drivers achieve 15–25% below EPA highway estimates in mixed driving conditions.
What is L/100km?
Liters per 100 kilometers (L/100km) is the fuel efficiency metric used in most countries outside the U.S. Unlike MPG, lower is better — a more efficient car uses fewer liters per 100 km. Convert: L/100km = 235.21 ÷ MPG. A 28 MPG car uses about 8.4 L/100km.
