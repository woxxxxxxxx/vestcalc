Frequently Asked Questions

How much does childcare cost on average?
Average annual childcare costs in the U.S. (2024): infant daycare: $12,000–$25,000/year; toddler daycare: $10,000–$20,000/year; full-time nanny: $35,000–$55,000/year (plus taxes). In major metros (NYC, SF, Boston), infant daycare often exceeds $30,000/year — more than in-state college tuition.
What is the Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit?
Working parents can claim the Child and Dependent Care Credit on federal taxes — up to 35% of $3,000 in qualifying expenses for one child ($6,000 for two+). This means up to $1,050–$2,100 in tax savings. Some employers also offer a Dependent Care FSA (up to $5,000 pre-tax) for additional savings.
Is a nanny cheaper than daycare?
Generally, no — for one child. A full-time nanny typically costs $35,000–$60,000/year (plus employer taxes), while center-based daycare averages $10,000–$25,000/year. However, for two or more children, a nanny can become cost-competitive since nanny costs do not double like daycare tuition does.
At what age can children start preschool?
Most preschools accept children at age 2.5–3 years (must be potty trained for many programs). Pre-K programs typically start at age 4. Head Start (for income-eligible families) provides free preschool starting at age 3. Some states offer universal Pre-K for all 4-year-olds.
