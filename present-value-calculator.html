Frequently Asked Questions

What is present value (PV)?
Present value is how much a future sum of money is worth today, accounting for the time value of money. If you will receive $10,000 in 5 years at an 8% discount rate, the PV is about $6,806 — what you would need to invest today to reach that amount.
What discount rate should I use?
Use a rate that reflects the opportunity cost of money — your required rate of return or expected investment return. Common choices: risk-free rate (U.S. Treasury yield) for safe cash flows, or 7–10% for equity-risk investments.
What is the difference between PV and NPV?
Present value discounts a single future amount to today. Net Present Value (NPV) sums the present values of multiple future cash flows, then subtracts the initial investment cost. NPV is used in investment analysis.
How does compounding frequency affect present value?
More frequent compounding discounts future cash flows more aggressively, resulting in a lower present value. Monthly compounding produces a slightly lower PV than annual compounding at the same nominal rate.
