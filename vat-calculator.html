Frequently Asked Questions

What is VAT?
Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax applied at each stage of production and distribution, based on the value added at each stage. Used by 170+ countries including all EU nations, it is collected at every point in the supply chain.
How do I add VAT to a price?
To add VAT: multiply the net price by (1 + VAT rate). Example: £100 × 1.20 = £120 at 20% UK VAT. The VAT amount alone is £100 × 0.20 = £20. Use this calculator to do it instantly for any country's rate.
How do I remove VAT from a price?
To extract VAT from an inclusive price: divide by (1 + VAT rate). Example: £120 ÷ 1.20 = £100 net price. The VAT paid was £120 − £100 = £20. This is the reverse-VAT (ex-VAT) calculation.
What is the difference between VAT and sales tax?
Sales tax is collected only at the final retail sale. VAT is collected at every production stage. Consumers pay a similar total under both systems, but VAT is harder to evade and is preferred internationally.
What are common VAT rates around the world?
Standard VAT rates: UK 20%, EU average ~21%, Australia 10% (GST), Canada 5% federal GST, India 18% GST, Japan 10%, New Zealand 15%. Many countries apply reduced rates to essentials like food, medicine, and children's clothing.
