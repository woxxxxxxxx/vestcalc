Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good annual investment return?
The S&P 500 has historically returned about 10% annually before inflation, or roughly 7% after. Stocks typically yield 7–10%, bonds 3–5%, and real estate 8–12% including rent income. Compare returns to the risk you are taking on.
What is the difference between nominal and real return?
Nominal return is the raw percentage gain without adjusting for inflation. Real return subtracts inflation to show your actual purchasing power increase. If an investment earned 8% and inflation was 3%, your real return was about 5%.
How does portfolio diversification reduce risk?
Diversification spreads investments across asset classes, sectors, and geographies. When some holdings fall, others may rise, smoothing overall volatility. It reduces unsystematic risk without necessarily reducing expected long-term returns.
What is the difference between total return and price return?
Price return measures only capital appreciation. Total return includes dividends and interest reinvested. Dividends can account for a significant portion of long-term stock returns, so total return gives the complete picture.
