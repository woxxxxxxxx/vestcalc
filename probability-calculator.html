Frequently Asked Questions

What is probability?
Probability = number of favorable outcomes ÷ total possible outcomes. It ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). A fair coin flip has P(heads) = 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%.
What is the difference between P(A and B) vs P(A or B)?
P(A and B) — both events occur simultaneously (intersection). For independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B). P(A or B) — at least one event occurs (union): P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B).
What is complementary probability?
The complement of event A is 'not A' — the probability that A does NOT happen. P(not A) = 1 − P(A). Useful when calculating 'at least one' problems: P(at least one) = 1 − P(none).
What is binomial probability?
Binomial probability calculates the chance of exactly k successes in n independent trials, each with probability p. Formula: P(X=k) = C(n,k) × p^k × (1−p)^(n−k). Example: probability of exactly 3 heads in 10 coin flips.
