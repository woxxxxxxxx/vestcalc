Frequently Asked Questions

What is dividend yield?
Dividend yield = annual dividend per share ÷ current share price × 100. A 3% yield means you earn $3 annually for every $100 invested. Sustainable yields of 2–4% are typical for quality companies; very high yields may signal risk.
How is dividend income taxed?
Qualified dividends (from U.S. companies held over 60 days) are taxed at long-term capital gains rates: 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income. Dividends held in a Roth IRA or 401(k) grow completely tax-free.
What is dividend reinvestment (DRIP)?
A Dividend Reinvestment Plan (DRIP) automatically uses dividend payments to purchase additional shares. Over decades, DRIP investing dramatically amplifies total returns through compounding — dividends generate their own dividends.
What makes a dividend sustainable?
Look at the payout ratio (dividends ÷ earnings). Below 60% generally indicates sustainability. Also check dividend growth history — companies that consistently raise dividends (Dividend Aristocrats) tend to be financially strong.
