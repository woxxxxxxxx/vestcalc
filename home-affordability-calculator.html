Frequently Asked Questions

How much house can I afford?
A general rule: keep your mortgage payment under 28% of gross monthly income and total debt payments under 36–43%. With a 20% down payment at 7% rate, $100,000 annual income can typically afford a $350,000–$400,000 home.
What is the 28/36 rule?
Lenders often use the 28/36 rule: your mortgage payment (including taxes and insurance) should not exceed 28% of gross monthly income, and total monthly debt payments should not exceed 36% of gross monthly income.
What does debt-to-income ratio (DTI) mean?
DTI = total monthly debt payments ÷ gross monthly income × 100. Most lenders want a back-end DTI below 43%. A lower DTI means more financial flexibility and usually results in better loan terms and lower interest rates.
Does home price or down payment matter more for affordability?
Both matter differently. A higher down payment reduces your monthly payment and eliminates PMI. Buying a less expensive home permanently lowers your payment. Combining both strategies maximizes long-term affordability.
