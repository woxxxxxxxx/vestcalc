Frequently Asked Questions

How is the due date calculated?
The standard method is Naegele's Rule: add 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). If your cycle is longer or shorter than 28 days, the due date is adjusted accordingly. Only about 5% of babies are born on their exact due date.
What is the typical range for delivery?
A full-term pregnancy ranges from 39–40 weeks. Babies born between 37–42 weeks are considered term. Births before 37 weeks are preterm; after 42 weeks is post-term. Most healthcare providers will consider induction around 41–42 weeks.
Can an ultrasound change my due date?
Yes — an early ultrasound (before 14 weeks) is more accurate than LMP for dating, especially if cycles are irregular. Ultrasound dating uses the size of the fetus to estimate gestational age. After 20 weeks, ultrasound dating becomes less precise.
What are the three trimesters?
The first trimester spans weeks 1–13 and involves critical organ development. The second trimester covers weeks 14–26 — often the most comfortable period. The third trimester is weeks 27–40+, when the baby gains most of its weight and prepares for birth.
