Frequently Asked Questions

How is age calculated?
Age is calculated by counting the full years, months, and days between your date of birth and the current (or target) date. A full year only counts when both the month and day of the anniversary have been reached in the current year.
Why does the number of days in months matter for age calculation?
Months have different lengths (28–31 days), so converting between months and days requires knowing which specific months are involved. This calculator properly accounts for varying month lengths and leap years.
What is the difference between chronological age and biological age?
Chronological age is the number of years since birth — what this calculator computes. Biological age reflects how your body has aged physiologically, which can differ significantly based on lifestyle, genetics, and health habits.
Can I calculate the age between two specific dates?
Yes — enter any past date as your date of birth and any future or past date as the target date. The calculator computes the elapsed time between the two dates, useful for project durations, anniversaries, or historical date differences.
