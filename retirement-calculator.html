Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need to retire?
A common rule is the 25× rule: save 25 times your expected annual expenses. This supports the 4% rule — withdrawing 4% annually, adjusted for inflation, which historically lasts 30+ years. For $60,000/year spending, target $1.5 million saved.
What is the 4% rule?
The 4% rule (Bengen rule) states that retirees can safely withdraw 4% of their portfolio in year one, then adjust for inflation annually, with high confidence the portfolio will last 30 years. Research shows it survives most historical market scenarios.
When should I start saving for retirement?
As early as possible. Investing $5,000/year starting at 25 (for 40 years at 8%) results in ~$1.4M by 65. Starting at 35 with the same contributions yields only ~$610K. Each decade of delay roughly halves the final balance.
What are the best accounts for retirement savings?
Priority: 1) 401(k) up to employer match (free money), 2) HSA if eligible (triple tax advantage), 3) Roth or traditional IRA ($7,000 limit in 2025), 4) Max out 401(k) ($23,500 limit), 5) Taxable brokerage for additional savings.
