Frequently Asked Questions

What is overtime pay?
Overtime pay is additional compensation for hours worked beyond a standard threshold — typically 40 hours per week in the U.S. The FLSA requires eligible employees to receive at least 1.5× their regular hourly rate for overtime hours.
Who is exempt from overtime?
Employees classified as exempt under the FLSA do not qualify for overtime. Common exemptions include executive, professional, and administrative employees earning over $684/week. Independent contractors are also exempt from overtime rules.
Is overtime calculated weekly or daily?
In the U.S., FLSA overtime is calculated weekly — more than 40 hours in a workweek triggers 1.5× pay. Some states like California also require overtime for more than 8 hours worked in a single day.
How does California overtime law differ?
California requires 1.5× pay for hours 8–12 in a single day, and 2× pay for hours beyond 12 in a day. The 7th consecutive workday in a workweek also triggers overtime at 1.5× for the first 8 hours and 2× beyond 8.
