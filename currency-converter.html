Frequently Asked Questions

What determines currency exchange rates?
Exchange rates are driven by supply and demand, influenced by interest rates, inflation, economic growth, political stability, and trade balances. Central bank policies can also significantly impact exchange rates in both directions.
What is the mid-market rate?
The mid-market rate is the midpoint between the buy and sell rates that banks trade with each other. It is the fairest rate available. Retail banks and exchange services typically charge a markup over this rate.
How often do exchange rates change?
Exchange rates fluctuate continuously — currency markets operate 24 hours a day, 5 days a week. Major rates can move by fractions of a percent within minutes during volatile economic or political events.
How can I get the best rate when traveling?
Use ATMs abroad with low foreign transaction fees, or cards with no foreign transaction fees. Avoid airport kiosks and hotel exchanges, which typically offer the worst rates and highest markups.
