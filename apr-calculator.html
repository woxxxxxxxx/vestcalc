Frequently Asked Questions

What is APR?
Annual Percentage Rate (APR) represents the true yearly cost of a loan — interest rate plus fees (origination fees, points, mortgage insurance). APR is always greater than or equal to the interest rate and allows fair comparison between loans.
Why is APR higher than the stated interest rate?
APR spreads loan fees over the loan term and adds them to the interest rate. A loan with a 6.5% rate but $3,000 in fees might have a 6.8% APR. The shorter the loan term, the more dramatically fees inflate the APR.
What is a good APR?
Good APRs vary by loan type. Mortgages: 6–8% (2025 market); personal loans: 8–15% for excellent credit; credit cards: 15–20% for good credit. Credit card APRs above 24% are typically considered high.
How does credit card APR work?
Credit card APR is yearly, but interest is calculated daily (APR ÷ 365 = daily rate). If you pay your full balance each month, you pay zero interest regardless of the APR. Carrying a balance causes daily compounding.
