Frequently Asked Questions

Is it financially better to rent or buy?
It depends on how long you plan to stay, local price-to-rent ratios, your down payment, and mortgage rates. In high-cost cities, renting and investing the difference can outperform buying. In affordable markets, buying often wins over 5+ years.
What is the price-to-rent ratio?
Price-to-rent ratio = home price ÷ annual rent. Below 15 generally favors buying; above 20 often favors renting. Above 25 means homes are expensive relative to rents, and renting is likely the better financial choice.
What hidden costs should I factor into homeownership?
Beyond the mortgage, include property taxes (1–2% annually), homeowner's insurance (0.5–1%), maintenance (1–2% per year), HOA fees if applicable, and selling costs (5–6% in agent commissions) when you move.
How long do I need to stay for buying to beat renting?
The break-even period accounts for closing costs, selling costs, opportunity cost of the down payment, and appreciation. In most markets, you need to stay 4–7 years for buying to mathematically beat renting.
