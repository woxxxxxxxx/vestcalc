Frequently Asked Questions

How large should my emergency fund be?
Most advisors recommend 3–6 months of essential living expenses. If you are self-employed, have variable income, or work in an unstable industry, aim for 6–12 months. Two-income households may need less than single-income ones.
Where should I keep my emergency fund?
Keep it in a high-yield savings account (HYSA) or money market account — liquid, FDIC-insured, and earning 4–5% APY. Avoid investing it in stocks; market downturns often coincide with job losses, exactly when you need the funds.
What qualifies as a financial emergency?
True emergencies include job loss, unexpected medical bills, major car or home repairs, or a family crisis. Planned expenses such as holidays or known car maintenance are not emergencies — budget for them separately as sinking funds.
How do I build an emergency fund quickly?
Redirect any windfall (tax refund, bonus) straight to savings. Cut discretionary spending temporarily. Set up automatic transfers on payday. Even $25–50 per week adds up to $1,300–$2,600 in a year.
