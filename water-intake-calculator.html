Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should I drink per day?
The National Academies recommends about 125 oz (3.7 liters) for men and 91 oz (2.7 liters) for women total daily water — including water from food (about 20%). A common estimate is 0.5 oz per pound of body weight as a baseline, adjusted upward for activity and climate.
Does coffee and tea count toward hydration?
Contrary to popular belief, caffeinated drinks like coffee and tea do count toward daily fluid intake. Mild diuretic effects are offset by the water content. However, alcohol is dehydrating and should not count toward your daily fluid goal.
How can I tell if I am drinking enough water?
The easiest way is urine color. Pale yellow (like lemonade) indicates good hydration. Dark yellow or amber means you need more water. Clear urine can signal over-hydration. Thirst is also a reliable indicator for most healthy adults.
Do I need to drink more water when exercising?
Yes. You lose fluid through sweat — roughly 16–32 oz per hour of moderate exercise. Drink water before, during, and after workouts. In hot conditions or during intense activity, consider electrolyte drinks to replace sodium and potassium lost in sweat.
