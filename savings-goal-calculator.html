Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I save each month?
A common guideline is the 50/30/20 rule: 50% of after-tax income for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings. For a specific goal, work backwards from the target amount, timeline, and expected interest rate.
What is a high-yield savings account?
A high-yield savings account (HYSA) typically offers 10–20x the interest of a traditional savings account. They are FDIC-insured and available at many online banks, significantly reducing the time needed to reach a savings goal.
Should I invest or save for my goal?
For goals under 3 years away, keep money in savings accounts or CDs for stability. For longer-term goals (3+ years), investing in index funds may generate higher returns, though with more short-term price risk.
How does compound interest help me reach goals faster?
Compound interest earns returns on both your principal and accumulated interest. The longer your timeline, the more dramatic the compounding effect. Starting even a few years earlier can result in tens of thousands of dollars more.
