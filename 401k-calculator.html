Frequently Asked Questions

What is a 401(k) plan?
A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement savings plan that lets employees contribute pre-tax income (traditional) or after-tax income (Roth 401k). Investments grow tax-deferred until withdrawal. Many employers offer matching contributions.
How much should I contribute to my 401(k)?
At minimum, contribute enough to capture your full employer match — that is an immediate 50–100% return on your money. The 2025 contribution limit is $23,500 ($31,000 if age 50+). Aim to save 10–15% of income for retirement total.
What is a 401(k) employer match?
Many employers match a percentage of your contributions — e.g., 50% match up to 6% of salary adds $0.50 for every $1 you contribute up to 6% of pay. This is effectively a pay raise. Always contribute at least enough to get the full match.
What happens to my 401(k) if I leave my job?
You own your vested balance. Options: leave it with the old employer, roll into a new employer's plan, roll into an IRA (most flexible), or cash out (not recommended — you will owe income tax plus a 10% early withdrawal penalty if under 59½).
