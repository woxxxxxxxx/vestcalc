Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost to hire movers?
Local moves typically cost $800–$2,500 for a 2-bedroom home (2–4 hours at $100–$150/hour). Long-distance moves run $2,500–$8,000+ depending on distance and volume. Cross-country moves for a 3-bedroom home average $4,000–$10,000. Always get at least 3 written quotes.
Is it cheaper to move yourself?
DIY moving with a rental truck saves 50–70% versus full-service movers but requires significant physical effort, time, and coordination. Truck rentals cost $200–$2,000+ depending on size and distance. Factor in fuel, packing supplies, time off work, and potential injury risk.
When is the cheapest time to move?
Weekdays are 15–25% cheaper than weekends. Late fall through winter (October–April) is the off-season with better rates and availability. Avoid moving at month-end when demand spikes. Book movers 4–8 weeks in advance for the best rates.
What should I look for in a moving company?
Verify they are licensed (check FMCSA for interstate movers), read reviews on multiple platforms, get binding estimates in writing, confirm they offer valuation coverage (basic liability is free but limited), and avoid companies that demand large deposits upfront.
