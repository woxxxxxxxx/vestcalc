Frequently Asked Questions

What is net worth?
Net worth = total assets − total liabilities. Assets include cash, investments, real estate, and property. Liabilities include mortgage, car loans, credit cards, and student loans. Positive net worth means you own more than you owe.
What is a good net worth by age?
Fidelity suggests: 1× your salary saved by 30, 3× by 40, 6× by 50, 8× by 60. The median U.S. net worth is around $87,000 (Federal Reserve 2022), a realistic benchmark for most households.
What assets should I include?
Include checking and savings accounts, retirement accounts (401k, IRA), brokerage accounts, real estate equity, vehicles, and business ownership stakes. Exclude depreciating consumer goods unless they have significant resale value.
How do I increase my net worth?
Increase assets by saving and investing consistently. Reduce liabilities by paying off high-interest debt aggressively. Avoid lifestyle inflation as income rises. Increasing income while keeping expenses stable is the most effective lever.
