Frequently Asked Questions

What is a mortgage?
A mortgage is a loan used to purchase real estate, where the property serves as collateral. You borrow from a lender and repay over a set term — typically 15 or 30 years — with monthly payments covering principal and interest.
How much down payment do I need?
Conventional loans typically require 3–20% down. Putting 20% down avoids Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). FHA loans allow as little as 3.5% down, while VA and USDA loans may require no down payment at all.
What is PMI?
Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is required on conventional loans when your down payment is less than 20%. It protects the lender if you default. PMI typically costs 0.5–1.5% of the loan amount per year and can be removed once you reach 20% equity.
What affects my mortgage interest rate?
Your credit score, loan-to-value ratio, loan term, property type, and market conditions all affect your rate. A higher credit score and larger down payment generally result in a lower interest rate.
Should I choose a 15-year or 30-year mortgage?
A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but much lower total interest. A 30-year mortgage offers lower payments with more flexibility. Choose 15-year if you can comfortably afford the payments; 30-year if you need cash flow room.
