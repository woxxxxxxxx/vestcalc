Frequently Asked Questions

What is a carbon footprint?
A carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gas emissions caused by an individual, event, organization, or product, expressed as CO₂ equivalent (CO₂e). The average American's carbon footprint is about 16 tons of CO₂e per year — one of the highest per capita in the world.
What is the global average carbon footprint?
The global average is about 4 tons of CO₂ per person per year. To limit global warming to 1.5°C, experts suggest the average per-capita carbon footprint must drop to under 2 tons by 2050. The U.S. average of 16 tons/year is roughly 4× the global average.
What has the biggest impact on my carbon footprint?
For most Americans: driving and flying (transportation) account for 28% of U.S. emissions; home energy use (heating, cooling, electricity) about 20%; diet, especially red meat consumption, contributes significantly. Switching to an electric vehicle and reducing flying typically have the highest individual impact.
How many trees offset 1 ton of CO₂?
A mature tree absorbs roughly 48 lbs (22 kg) of CO₂ per year. To offset 1 metric ton (2,205 lbs) of CO₂, you need approximately 45 trees growing for a year. Tree planting is a valuable but partial solution — reducing emissions at the source remains more effective.
