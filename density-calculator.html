Frequently Asked Questions

What is the density formula?
Density (D) = Mass (m) ÷ Volume (V). Rearranged: Mass = Density × Volume; Volume = Mass ÷ Density. Density tells you how much mass is packed into a given volume — a key property for identifying materials.
What is the density of water?
Pure water at 4°C has a density of exactly 1.000 g/cm³ (1,000 kg/m³). This is the reference used to define specific gravity. Above 4°C water becomes less dense; ice is about 0.917 g/cm³ — why ice floats.
How do you measure volume for density calculations?
For regular shapes: use geometry (V = L×W×H for rectangles, πr²h for cylinders). For irregular objects: use Archimedes' water displacement method — submerge the object and measure the volume of water displaced.
What is specific gravity?
Specific gravity = density of substance ÷ density of water (1 g/cm³). It's dimensionless and tells you if something floats (SG < 1) or sinks (SG > 1) in water. Gold SG ≈ 19.3, wood SG ≈ 0.5.
