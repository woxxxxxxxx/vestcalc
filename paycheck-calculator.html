Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between gross pay and net pay?
Gross pay is total earnings before any deductions. Net pay (take-home) is what you receive after federal and state income taxes, Social Security, Medicare, health insurance premiums, and 401(k) contributions are deducted.
What is FICA tax?
FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act) funds Social Security and Medicare. Employees pay 6.2% for Social Security (on wages up to $168,600 in 2025) and 1.45% for Medicare — a combined 7.65%. Employers match these amounts.
How do I change my federal withholding?
Complete a new Form W-4 and submit it to your employer. More allowances reduce withholding (larger paychecks, possible tax bill at filing). Fewer allowances increase withholding (smaller paychecks, likely a larger refund).
Why does my paycheck change when I get a raise?
A raise may push more income into a higher marginal tax bracket, increasing withholding on the additional income. Pre-tax benefits like 401(k) contributions also recalculate when your pay changes.
