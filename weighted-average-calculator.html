Frequently Asked Questions

What is a weighted average?
A weighted average gives different importance (weight) to each value. Formula: Σ(value × weight) ÷ Σ(weights). A regular average treats all values equally; a weighted average accounts for the relative significance of each value.
When should I use weighted average vs simple average?
Use weighted average when values have different importance or frequency. Examples: GPA (credit hours as weights), investment portfolio returns (dollar amounts as weights), survey results (response frequency as weights).
Do my weights need to add up to 100?
No. Weights can be any positive numbers — the calculator normalizes them automatically. Whether you enter weights as 30/70 or 3/7 or 0.3/0.7, the result is the same weighted average.
How is weighted average used in finance?
Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) weights debt and equity by their proportions in a company's capital structure. Portfolio returns are weighted by asset value. Bond yields are weighted by duration or face value.
