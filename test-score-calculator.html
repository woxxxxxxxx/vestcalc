Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my test score percentage?
Divide the number of correct answers by the total number of questions, then multiply by 100. Example: 38 correct out of 50 questions = 38/50 × 100 = 76%.
How many questions can I miss and still pass?
To find the maximum you can miss: (1 – pass threshold) × total questions. To pass at 70% on a 50-question test: you need at least 35 correct, so you can miss up to 15 questions.
What is a passing score?
Passing thresholds vary. Most academic courses: 60–70%. Professional licensing exams (bar, nursing, CPA): often 70–75%. Standardized tests like the SAT/ACT use scaled scores rather than simple percentages.
How do I calculate a partial-credit test score?
Multiply each question's earned points by its point value, sum all earned points, divide by the total possible points, and multiply by 100. This calculator handles uniform point values — for varied points, use the Grade Calculator.
