Frequently Asked Questions

What is the speed distance time formula?
The three core formulas: Speed = Distance ÷ Time; Distance = Speed × Time; Time = Distance ÷ Speed. Remember with the triangle method: cover the variable you want, and the other two show the operation (× or ÷).
How do I convert mph to km/h?
Multiply mph by 1.60934 to get km/h. Example: 60 mph × 1.60934 = 96.56 km/h. Quick estimate: multiply by 1.6. To convert km/h to mph, divide by 1.60934 (multiply by 0.6214).
How do I calculate travel time?
Time = Distance ÷ Speed. Example: driving 240 miles at 60 mph takes 240/60 = 4 hours. In km: 400 km at 100 km/h = 4 hours. Add extra time for stops, traffic, and rest breaks.
What is average speed?
Average speed = total distance ÷ total time. It's NOT the average of two speeds unless the time intervals are equal. If you drive 60 mph for 1 hour and 30 mph for 1 hour, average speed = (60+30)/2 = 45 mph. But if distances are equal, average = 2/(1/60+1/30) = 40 mph.
